Why Forge is different

Seven questions that change
everything

AI gives you correct numbers and wrong conclusions — unless it knows what the numbers mean. Forge asks the questions that transform generic analysis into domain-specific insight.

Question 1
What is this data about?
Tells the AI what domain it's operating in. Without this, it applies generic patterns to specialist problems.
Question 2
Where does this data come from?
Source context changes how anomalies are interpreted. KoboToolbox field data isn't the same as a CRM export.
Question 3
What do status, category or flag columns mean?
"Flagged" means requires review — not invalid. Without this answer, AI recommends deleting everything that's flagged.
Question 4
What are the normal or expected values?
Defines the baseline for anomaly detection. A 42-million-hectare field is obviously wrong. Without context, the AI can't know that.
Question 5
What decision will this analysis support?
Shapes the output format. A deletion decision needs a different structure than an audit report or a correction workflow.
Question 6
Is there anything you already know or suspect?
Primes the AI with domain knowledge. "We suspect a coordinate order bug" is worth 10 prompts of generic exploration.
Question 7 — The one that prevents the worst errors
Are stored or reference values independently verified?
Prevents circular error conclusions — where both the calculated and stored values are wrong for the same reason. This question catches what no generic AI analysis ever catches on its own. Included in Decide Faster and above.

Common questions

FAQ

What counts as a synthesis?
+
A synthesis is any time you click "Run Synthesis", "Best Answer", or "Analyze →". Getting perspectives (the seven AI responses) is always free and unlimited. You only use a synthesis credit when Forge combines those responses into a single decision-ready output.
Do I need my own AI subscriptions?
+
No. Forge uses its own access to all seven providers (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Mistral, DeepSeek, Perplexity, Grok) — your Forge subscription is all you need. If you already have subscriptions and want to use your own accounts via the browser extension, you can do that too.
What is white-label and who is it for?
+
White-label means your exported reports carry your name and company instead of Forge's. It's for analysts, consultants, and advisors who deliver work to clients and need the output to look like theirs — not like it came from an AI tool. Takes two minutes to set up on first export.
Can I switch plans or cancel anytime?
+
Yes. You can upgrade, downgrade, or cancel at any time from your profile page. If you cancel, you keep your current plan until the end of the billing period. No cancellation fees.
Is my data private?
+
Forge is operated by Xencore Global GmbH under Swiss jurisdiction. Your prompts and AI responses are sent to the AI providers you use, under their respective privacy policies. We do not train models on your data and do not retain your analysis content after your session ends.
What's the difference between Forge and just using ChatGPT or Claude directly?
+
One question reaches seven models simultaneously — you see where they agree and where they diverge, which tells you something no single AI can tell you. The synthesis layer then combines their strongest elements into one answer. The context questions for Excel analysis are also Forge-specific: they're the mechanism that transforms generic AI analysis into domain-specific, decision-ready output.
