The problem with AI today

You're getting one
answer. You need
the full picture.

Ask the same question to multiple AI models and you often get different conclusions. Some are shallow, some are insightful, and some contradict each other. Without seeing the full picture, you can't know which to trust.

⚠

Inconsistent answers

Ask the same question to Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini. You'll get three different conclusions — with no way to know which is most reliable without reading all three yourself.

◎

Hidden blind spots

Every AI model has biases baked in from its training data. Relying on a single model means inheriting its blind spots. You don't know what you don't know.

↻

No synthesis layer

Even if you read all seven responses manually, combining them into one coherent decision-ready output requires effort most people skip — and that's where the value lives.
