Perspectives

Ask one question and get responses from all seven AIs simultaneously. The foundation of everything in Forge.

Ask → Get perspectives → Compare → Synthesize
  • Select which AIs respond using the provider chips
  • Responses stream in as they arrive — no waiting for all seven
  • Click any chip to expand the full response
  • Use follow-up chips to ask connected questions in context
  • The synthesis strip shows consensus % and a plain-English summary

Synthesis

Seven analysis modes that combine AI responses into structured, decision-ready outputs — from a best-of-best answer to a divergence map.

Perspectives → Synthesis → Export
  • Best-of-Best — combines the strongest elements from all responses
  • Executive Summary — direct answer and key insights only
  • Consensus Map — where AIs agree and how strongly
  • Divergence Spotlight — highlights where AIs differ and why
  • Quality Scorecard — scores each response on clarity and depth

Common questions
Quick answers.
Why do I need Forge if I already have ChatGPT and Claude?
+
One question reaches seven models at once, and you see where they agree and diverge — which tells you something no single AI can tell you. High agreement means the answer is robust. High divergence means the question is genuinely contested and you should read carefully before deciding. The synthesis layer then builds on that signal to produce a single output calibrated to the areas of agreement.
What counts as a synthesis and when does my count reset?
+
A synthesis is any time you run a synthesis mode (Best-of-Best, Executive Summary, Consensus Map, etc.) or click "Analyze →" in Excel Intelligence. Getting perspectives — the seven AI responses themselves — is always free and unlimited. Your monthly synthesis count resets on the first of each month. You can see your current count and reset date on your Profile under Synthesis Usage.
Why do the seven context questions in Excel Intelligence matter so much?
+
Without context, AI applies generic patterns to specialist problems. In a real test on the same dataset, answering no questions produced a recommendation to delete 371 valid records. Answering all seven produced a document submittable to a carbon credit verifier — naming specific field IDs, detecting circular errors, and generating a three-phase action plan. The questions are not prompts. They are a structured intake that gives the AI the domain knowledge it needs to interpret your data correctly.
Do I need my own AI subscriptions?
+
No. Forge uses its own backend access to all seven providers — Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Mistral, DeepSeek, Perplexity, and Grok. Your Forge subscription is all you need. If you already have your own subscriptions and prefer to route through your own accounts via the browser extension, you can do that too — but it is not required.
What is white-label and how do I set it up?
+
White-label means your exported reports carry your name and company instead of Forge's. It is available on Work Like a Pro and above. When you export a document for the first time, you will be prompted to enter your name, company name, and optionally a logo. This takes about two minutes and is saved for all future exports. You can update it at any time from Profile under Account Settings.
How do I delete my account or download my data?
+
Go to Profile → Account Settings. You can request a data export or close your account from there. Account closure deletes your Prompt Library immediately and all personal data within 30 days. If you have an active paid subscription, it will remain active until the end of the current billing period before the account is closed.
Something is broken or I found a bug. How do I report it?
+
Email support@projectcoachai.com with a description of what happened, what you expected to happen, and the page you were on. Screenshots are very helpful. Critical issues are resolved within 24 hours. For less urgent bugs, we aim to respond within 48 hours and fix within the next release.
